Trust

The road to being 100% secure is twice as long as the road to 99%.
This tool is for those of you who want to go that extra mile.

How much trust are you placing in your environment?

I'm just going to give it to you straight - there are MANY risks to your private keys that exist. If you're willing to assume that your computer and network are clean and that this tool is not evil, feel free to skip this section and continue on to Wallet Setup.

Full Trust: You are fully trusting this element to not be spying on you or collecting key data
Some Trust: You are partially trusting this element to not be spying on you or collecting key data. Some vulnerabilities have been protected against, but others exist.
Zero Trust: You are giving no trust to this element. It could be compromised and malicious and there would still be no way for your coins to be taken.

Risks - Detailed

Infections

Spyware, Malware, Trojans, Viruses, Keyloggers, Root-kits

An infection could be watching your keystrokes, clipboard or your screen.
Take your computer offline
An infection could be watching your keystrokes, clipboard or your screen, storing them on a hard drive while offline, to be broadcast later.

Take your computer offline permanently OR switch to a Live CD / USB

Your Network

Infected network nodes, Network intruders

An infected network device or a network intruder could intercept print jobs as they are sent.
Connect your printer to your computer directly

Your Printer

Printer history

If your printer keeps a history of print jobs, they could be taken by anyone with network access to it.
Connect your printer to your computer directly OR Ensure your printer does not save its history
If your printer keeps a history of print jobs, once the printer is brought online, the print jobs could be taken by anyone with network access to it.
Connect your printer to your computer directly, and never connect it to a network OR Ensure your printer does not save its history

Your Browser

Malicious browser addons could be tracking everything that gets rendered on your screen.
Take your computer offline

Take your computer offline permanently

Private Key

Import / Generate

A private key consists of a series of 32 bytes (256 bits). Your coin address is produced from this.

Paste a private key exported from your coin wallet software into the box below or click on Generate to generate a new private key from the Random Number Generator pool.

(Hex Private Keys, Wallet Import Format (WIF), compressed or uncompressed are all supported)

RNG Entropy

Having a good secure Random Number Generator (RNG) is essential to creating secure paper wallets.

'Entropy' is random or semi-random, unpredictable data.
This tool's RNG uses system, time, and browser data to fill the pool initially, then adds at least coordinates of mouse movement to that.

The keys produced by this tool aren't predictable in any way.








This is the RNG data pool. As you move your mouse around the screen, the data is further randomized.
